The Data Protection Foundation is dedicated to creating a sustainable, transparent, equitable, and innovative modern data economy which respects the data rights of individuals. Our mission is to enable people and communities to act collectively to protect their data rights.

The DPF is a non-partisan, not-for-profit research organisation developing infrastructural jurisprudence designed to enable collective litigation against businesses in the modern data economy that infringe on personal data rights.

These businesses operate opaque, extractive, inequitable business models predicated on invasive and extensive profiling and content targeting of individuals. Not only is this against the law, it also damages individuals and societies both overtly and insidiously. Collective litigations resulting in large damages awards are the only viable mechanism that can change the damaging business models of the modern data economy.

Our founding governing board is entirely unremunerated and comprises Baroness Beeban Kidron OBE (Founder and chair, 5Rights Foundation), and Dr Vaughn Tan PhD.
